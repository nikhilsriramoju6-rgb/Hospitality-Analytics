# Hospitality-Analytics

## Project Objective
The Hospitality Analytics project is an end-to-end Business Intelligence solution designed to analyze hotel booking data and provide actionable insights into hotel performance, revenue generation, occupancy, and customer behavior.
Using SQL for data analysis and Tableau for interactive dashboards, this project transforms raw hospitality data into meaningful visualizations that help stakeholders make informed business decisions.

## Questions(KPI's)
1.Total RevenueThe total income generated from all successful bookings and stays.

2.Occupancy RateThe percentage of available rooms that are occupied during a specific time period.

3.Cancellation RateThe proportion of bookings that were canceled before check-in.

4.Total BookingsThe total number of reservations made, regardless of their final status.

5.Utilized CapacityA measure of how effectively the available room capacity is being used.

6.Trend AnalysisObserving and evaluating how key metrics like revenue and occupancy change over time.

7.Weekday & Weekend Revenue and BookingComparison of booking volume and revenue generation between weekdays and weekends.

8.Revenue by State & HotelBreakdown of total revenue generated, segmented by hotel location and property.

9.Class Wise RevenueRevenue segmented by room category (e.g., standard, deluxe, suite).

10.Checked Out / Cancel / No ShowStatus-based categorization of bookings, indicating guest behavior or booking outcome.

11.Weekly Trend – Key MetricsWeekly insights into key performance indicators such as revenue, bookings, and occupancy.

## Insights / Suggestions & Recommendations 

Business Insights
1. Revenue Performance
* A small number of hotels contribute a significant share of the total revenue, indicating high-performing properties.
* Luxury room categories generate higher revenue per booking due to their premium pricing.
* Revenue fluctuates throughout the year, with noticeable peaks during holidays and vacation seasons.
2. Occupancy Trends
* Occupancy rates are consistently higher on weekends compared to weekdays.
* Premium hotels maintain relatively stable occupancy, while budget hotels experience larger seasonal variations.
* Certain cities consistently achieve higher occupancy, reflecting stronger travel demand.
3. Booking Behavior
* Most customers prefer booking through online travel platforms rather than direct hotel channels.
* Last-minute bookings increase during weekends and festive periods.
* Longer stays contribute significantly to total revenue despite representing a smaller percentage of bookings.
4. Cancellation Analysis
* Cancellation rates vary across booking platforms, suggesting differences in customer commitment and platform policies.
* High cancellation rates negatively impact occupancy forecasting and revenue planning.
* Properties with flexible cancellation policies tend to experience more cancellations.

Business Recommendations
1. Reduce Booking Cancellations
* Introduce attractive non-refundable booking options with discounts.
* Send automated reminders before check-in to reduce no-shows.
* Analyze platforms with high cancellation rates and revise booking policies accordingly.
2. Optimize Dynamic Pricing
* Implement demand-based pricing during weekends, holidays, and peak seasons.
* Offer promotional pricing during low-demand periods to improve occupancy.
* Regularly review competitor pricing to stay competitive.
3. Increase Direct Bookings
* Offer exclusive discounts, loyalty rewards, or complimentary services for customers who book directly through the hotel website.
* Improve the online booking experience with a user-friendly website and mobile optimization.
4. Improve Occupancy During Off-Peak Periods
* Launch seasonal promotions and staycation packages.
* Partner with travel agencies and corporate clients to attract consistent bookings.
* Create bundled offers that include dining, spa, or local experiences.
5. Enhance Customer Retention
* Develop a loyalty program with personalized rewards for repeat guests.
* Use guest booking history to deliver personalized promotions and recommendations.
* Collect post-stay feedback and act on recurring concerns to improve guest satisfaction.

Strategic Suggestions

1 Implement predictive analytics to forecast occupancy and demand.

2 Build customer segmentation models based on travel purpose, booking behavior, and spending patterns.

3 Develop executive dashboards that refresh automatically for real-time performance monitoring.

4 Track key metrics such as ADR, RevPAR, Occupancy Rate, and Cancellation Rate regularly to support decision-making.

5 Integrate customer review sentiment analysis to identify service improvement opportunities.

6 Explore machine learning models to predict booking cancellations and recommend retention strategies.

## Conclusion 

This Hospitality Analytics project demonstrates how data analytics can be leveraged to improve decision-making in the hospitality industry. By integrating SQL for data analysis and Tableau for interactive dashboarding, the project transforms raw hotel booking data into meaningful business insights.

The analysis highlights key performance metrics such as Occupancy Rate, Average Daily Rate (ADR), Revenue per Available Room (RevPAR), Booking Trends, and Cancellation Rate, enabling stakeholders to monitor hotel performance and identify areas for improvement. Interactive dashboards provide a comprehensive view of revenue, occupancy, customer behavior, and booking patterns, making it easier to uncover trends and support strategic decisions.

Overall, this project showcases practical skills in SQL, Tableau, data modeling, KPI development, and business intelligence while addressing real-world challenges in the hospitality sector. It demonstrates the ability to convert raw data into actionable insights that support operational efficiency, revenue growth, and better customer experiences, making it a strong addition to a data analytics.
